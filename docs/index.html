About the Game

Our VR game offers an immersive experience where players navigate the world as a squirrel and a lumberjack. You'll learn about the behaviors of squirrels, including their nut-burying habits, and how these actions contribute to forest health and climate change mitigation. You can also play as a lumberjack, clearing land responsibly while learning about sustainable forestry.

Squirrels & Climate Change

Squirrels play a vital role in forest regeneration and climate mitigation through their seed-burying behaviors, while lumberjacks highlight human interaction with forests. Together, they demonstrate the balance of nature and sustainable practices.

  • Forest Regeneration: Squirrels bury nuts and seeds, some of which germinate and grow into new trees, aiding natural reforestation.
  • Climate Change Mitigation: New tree growth supports carbon sequestration, absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and helping combat climate change.
  • The Lumberjack's Role: Sustainable land use practices help preserve forest ecosystems while ensuring the responsible harvesting of timber.

Squirrel Species and Their Ecological Impact

There are many species of squirrels around the world, each playing a vital role in forest ecosystems. Some examples include:

  • Eastern Gray Squirrel: Known for their adaptability, they help in forest regeneration by burying seeds and nuts, some of which sprout into trees.
  • Red Squirrel: Found across Europe and North America, they contribute to seed dispersal, promoting plant diversity.
  • Flying Squirrel: These nocturnal creatures are excellent at spreading fungi spores, essential for forest health.

Deforestation & Climate Change

Deforestation contributes to nearly 15% of global greenhouse gas emissions. This impacts wildlife habitats, human livelihoods, and accelerates climate change.

Responsible forest management and reforestation efforts can help restore ecosystems and mitigate the effects of climate change. Together with the efforts of squirrels, we can make a significant impact on climate change.

Sustainable Forestry Practices

Sustainable forestry practices aim to balance the need for timber, biodiversity conservation, and ecosystem health. These practices include:

  • Selectively Cutting Trees: Ensuring that only mature trees are cut down to allow new growth.
  • Reforestation: Planting multiple saplings for every tree removed to help maintain the balance of the ecosystem.
  • Protecting Habitats: Sustainable forestry also aims to protect the natural habitats of wildlife, including squirrels, ensuring biodiversity.

Development Process

The game was built using Unity Engine with a focus on creating an immersive and educational experience. Key steps in the development process included:

  • Researching the ecological impact of squirrels and lumberjacks on forests.
  • Designing 3D models and animations to simulate the behaviors of squirrels and the actions of lumberjacks.
  • Incorporating educational content to enhance player understanding of sustainable practices.
  • Testing the game for smooth performance and user engagement.

This development journey aimed to create a balance between fun gameplay and impactful learning.
